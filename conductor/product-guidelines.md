# Product Guidelines

## Tone & Voice

- **Formal & Scholarly:** The application should maintain a professional and authoritative tone, appropriate for religious jurisprudence (fiqh). Content should be presented with scholarly clarity to instill confidence in the accuracy of the calculations.

## Language & Terminology

- **Indonesian with Glosses:** The primary language of the application is Indonesian. All Arabic fiqh terms must be accompanied by clear Indonesian translations or explanations.
- **Religious Terms Maintained:** Key terms such as _Mudd_, _Fidyah_, and _Madzhab Syafii_ should be used directly to maintain religious context, followed by their Indonesian meanings.
- **Explanatory Style:** Each calculation step and ruling should be supported by brief, clear explanations (dalil) in Indonesian to educate the user.

## Design Aesthetic

- **Traditionally Inspired UI:** The visual design should incorporate subtle Islamic art elements (e.g., geometric patterns or calligraphy) to create a respectful and culturally resonant environment.
- **Visual Balance:** Use a clean, organized layout that balances traditional motifs with modern usability.

## Accessibility

- **Large Legible Fonts:** Prioritize readability by using large, high-quality fonts to ensure the application is usable by all age groups.
- **Standard Web Accessibility:** Follow best practices for screen reader compatibility and keyboard navigation.
- **High Contrast Mode:** Provide a high-contrast option to ensure legibility in various lighting conditions.
